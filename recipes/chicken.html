Chicken

Description

Chicken has a reputation for being dry. This is not really the bird's fault; rather, it is a skill issue on behalf of the chef. Searing chicken locks in the juices, producing a tasty, moist cut instead of a rubbery, stringy one. Level up your chicken game with this dead simple recipe.

Note: this recipe is copied more or less verbatim from Simply Whisked. All credit goes to the original author.

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts (about 1 lb)
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1/4 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 to 1 tsp coarse salt
  • black pepper (to taste)

Instructions

  1. Pound chicken breast to a 1" thickness. Season with salt, pepper, and garlic powder.
  2. Heat a large skillet to medium-high. Add olive oil and let it get hot.
  3. Add chicken to the skillet, cooking for 4 min to 5 min per side, turning once, until the chicken has reached an internal temperature of 165 °F.
